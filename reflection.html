GROUP REFLECTION

In Assignment 3, we had split RHAMJET into two teams: research team and tech team. Having these two teams allowed individuals to focus on their component that they preferred and help others in their smaller team. Another good aspect in working through this assignment was the fact that a majority of RHAMJET were taking on more responsibilities than they were required to. This was beneficial as it reduced workload from another team member so they could focus more specifically and openly to the task they were working on. An improvement that was made from assignment 2 was setting deadlines on when certain things were due. This encouraged each team member to know and understand the pace and time needed to get their task done.

Even though having split RHAMJET into two teams had good benefits, it also brought communication issues between them. Throughout these last few weeks either team had a minimal understanding on what the other team was working on. This majorly came aware where the research team wanted to do some tasks such as the plans and progress for the tech team. When separating each task between the research team and tech team we didn’t correctly assume the amount of work each team got. We originally set work to what tasks had more research to the research team and the more tech side of the report was given to the tech team. However, as time went on, we had realised that the tech team had more work in comparison to the research team. The research team had their tasks done in the early weeks of the assignment. Despite this the research team took on more responsibilities to help the tech team finish their sections in the report. But for next time, we should appropriately measure the number of tasks given to each team and individually.

RHAMJET was surprised on the difficultly that software development brought while implementing our deliverables. We had allocated Jamie and Abel to work on our deliverables however this was regretful as these team members had put in so much effort and time into. Both had to learn a new programming language before they could work on the deliverable. So, we all realised that having two people working on programming the application was not enough and that more team members should have been in the process of developing the application.

RHAMJET learnt that having set a soft deadline led to more efficiency in each team member to get required things done and it also made our final week less busy compared to our last week of assignment 2. We learnt on how to work in a group through a different approach which we can take into consideration in our future life.

I myself was part of the writing team, so after I finished it was decided that I should also work on another deliverable, which was a wise idea. A thing I’ve learned during this assignment was finding out the importance of delegating different tasks to different people. This allows for an efficient workflow and lets people work to their strengths.
