GROUP REFLECTION

RHAMJET kept to the biweekly meeting requirements. No team member missed more than two meetings, and everyone made as much effort to attend, even if they had personal matters to attend to. Work was allocated in the first week and was uploaded to the MS Teams shared doc promptly for peer review. When we originally allocated tasks, we found that some of our team members had too much work on their hand, therefore we had to reallocate the tasks equally. Ultimately, we did well splitting workload fairly and ensuring everyone contributed equally. A progress update was included in the agenda every week, so we were always up to date on each other’s situations.

An aspect RHAMJET can improve on is more effectively managing our time. Initially we set no deadlines until late in the assignment, therefore next time we should set deadlines at the very beginning of the assignment. Group meetings were always very informal, and while we did our writing and team meetings on MS Teams, we also had a RHAMJET Discord. In communication we were often side-tracked by socializing. While this helped the group harmony and having a clear communication, it affected workflow and we still had everyone had different views on the project idea. We didn’t catch on to everyone’s confusion regarding the project idea until the later weeks. In future we should check in on each other not just for the work we’re personally responsible for, but that everyone has a clear understanding of each other’s work and what’s going on in the project holistically.

No one in RHAMJET liked the meeting requirements initially. We were all surprised by how helpful they were with getting progress updates from each member on the individual tasks and discussing different components of the assignment. The meetings worked to hold us accountable. We found as a group we got quite a bit of work done through having an equal balance of formality and informality to keep our work on progress, which was also unexpected.

Ultimately, RHAMJET has learned that being in a group is fun when each team member is cohesively and consistently contributing. It made things easier once we knew each other’s strengths and weaknesses where we could work around it. We allocated tasks in accordance with each member of RHAMJET’s strengths. We learned skills and techniques about working in groups that we can all take into later courses and future careers.

ELLIOT

One of the main gripes back in Assignment 2 was a lack of a clear deadline. To circumvent this, at the beginning of this project we assigned roles and responsibilities to every team member, and set up some deadlines through the use of an external app, Trello. However, the use of this app fell off during the later weeks. There was also an issue of some people having a little more to do on their hands. What I didn’t expect was the amount of work that we required to do. People in the tech department seemed to have a disproportionate amount of work to complete, needing to learn entirely new software within the span of 3 weeks and to deliver a presentable artifact in time, not to mention having to do some writing up for their parts. In comparison, the research department finished up their sections relatively quickly, so they needed to chip in to help with the tech people. I myself was part of the writing team, so after I finished it was decided that I should also work on another deliverable, which was a wise idea. A thing I’ve learned during this assignment was finding out the importance of delegating different tasks to different people. This allows for an efficient workflow and lets people work to their strengths.
